I found a poem that defines what a community means to me. The poem is called Handfuls by Hua Xi:



“I toss a handful into the air.
A handful of nonspecific stuff.
What is this that my hands are tossing?
Im tossing handfuls of snow into the air.
Where am I getting all this snow on a summers day?
It must come from somewhere inside me.
Im tossing handfuls of somewhere everywhere, all the way
up into the air, and its flitting down and amassing
atop the immaculate townships.
Tin roofs. Church steeples. Lines of parked cars.
Summer is a pure lone mountain.
Somehow, a winter flowers against an enormous blue loneliness
as a figure wilts far below and wonders,
How can snow fall without falling in love?
Wherever I go, my furthest thoughts are lightly billowing.
Whatever is buried within me, I keep
pulling out in tufts.
I hope that when I feel cold, you can feel what I feel
but without feeling any cold.
Because I have struggled to do so,
I choose to believe that
not all sadness comes from somewhere.
The sadness that comes from somewhere drifts down
and mixes with the sadness that isnt from anywhere.
All of us are ordinary people. None of us
can escape the difficult nature
of being thrown away
by a warm afternoon in winter.”
Why did poems connect with the word community?

Handfuls by Hua Xi To me, I believe that the poem I chose connected with the word community because it talks about the person having a time of his life in the snow even if none of the people that he described as ordinary could not escape the “difficult nature of being thrown away by a warm afternoon winter.”
They struggle together through the winter as they look out for one another.
Those quotes resonate with me because it's allowing me to emphasize the shared human experience, the vulnerability of "ordinary" life, and the act of collective emotional labor in the face of loneliness or suffering, so even if I'm all alone, there are still people deep within my heart sharing the same empthy with me, despite not being there for me physically.
Also, it's a reminder to me that everyone that I have known and loved are also carrying their own "handfuls" of inner weather.
That weakness and vulnerability in emotion is something I couldn't escape from due to it being part of an ordinary human's experiences, which is life. Lastly, for me personally, I believe the word community means that we are one big family that looks out for one another whenever its a blackout or strong snowstorm, we always have our backs even if we don't know each other all that much.
